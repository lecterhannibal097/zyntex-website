Introducing Security Circle: Build Your Trust Network

One of the most powerful features in Zyntex is the Security Circle — a trust-based network mechanic that permanently boosts your mining rate by up to 25% and forms the foundation of how Zyntex validates its human user base. In this comprehensive guide, we will explain what a Security Circle is, why it exists, how trust networks work in decentralized systems, how to build yours step by step, and everything you need to know to get the most from this feature.

What Is a Security Circle and Why Does It Exist?

A Security Circle is a group of up to 5 people you personally know and trust, whom you invite into your inner network on Zyntex. Once they accept your invitation, each member contributes a permanent +5% boost to your mining rate for as long as they remain active on the platform.

The Security Circle exists for reasons that go much deeper than just a bonus mechanism. In any decentralized token ecosystem, one of the hardest problems to solve is fake account inflation. Without verification, bad actors can create hundreds of bot accounts that mine tokens fraudulently, diluting the value of tokens earned by real, legitimate users.

Traditional crypto projects solve this with expensive hardware requirements — if mining costs money, bots are not economically viable. Mobile mining eliminates the hardware barrier, which is great for accessibility but creates a new challenge: how do you verify that miners are real humans?

The Security Circle is part of the answer. When you add someone to your circle and they add you back, you are creating a mutual trust attestation — effectively vouching that the other person is a real human you know. This web of mutual trust relationships creates a social graph that makes bot farming extremely difficult. A bot cannot build a genuine circle of five trusted humans. Real users can. The Security Circle therefore serves as both a reward for genuine participation and a defense mechanism for the network's integrity.

How Trust Networks Work in Decentralized Mining

The concept of a trust network in the context of blockchain and decentralized tokens is not new. The idea was popularized at scale by projects like Pi Network, which used a similar inner circle mechanic to distinguish human users from automated accounts. Zyntex builds on this model with its own implementation tailored for the ZYM ecosystem.

In a trust network, each user is represented as a node, and each Security Circle connection is a link between nodes. The more links a node has — meaning the more trusted relationships it has established — the more it is considered a legitimate participant. Isolated nodes (accounts with no circle connections) are statistically much more likely to be fraudulent or abandoned accounts.

When Zyntex evaluates the health of its network, the density and quality of Security Circle connections is one of the signals used. This is why building your Security Circle is not just about the personal mining boost — it contributes to the overall legitimacy and value of the ZYM token itself. A network where all users have verified trust connections is a network where ZYM has genuine scarcity, because bots and fake accounts cannot participate at full capacity.

Step-by-Step Guide to Building Your Security Circle

Building your Security Circle is straightforward, but there are nuances to doing it effectively. Follow these steps:

  1. Open the Zyntex app and navigate to the Home screen.
  2. Look for the Security Circle card — it shows your current circle status, including how many slots are filled and your current mining boost from the circle.
  3. Tap Add Member. You will be taken to a search screen where you can look up other Zyntex users by their username.
  4. Search for the person you want to add. Make sure you search by their exact username — Zyntex usernames are unique, so there should be no ambiguity.
  5. Tap their profile and select Send Circle Invite. They will receive a push notification and can accept or decline your invitation.
  6. Once they accept, their +5% boost is immediately added to your mining rate. You should see your displayed mining rate increase in real time on your dashboard.
  7. Repeat until all 5 slots in your Security Circle are filled.

🔒 Important: The Security Circle is built on the concept of genuine trust. Only add people you actually know personally — friends, family, colleagues, or classmates who are real Zyntex users. Adding strangers from the internet defeats the purpose of the trust network and may have implications for your account's standing in future verification processes.

How the 25% Mining Boost Is Calculated

The math behind the Security Circle boost is transparent and straightforward. Each active member you add to your circle contributes exactly +5% to your base mining rate. With 5 members, you receive 5 x 5% = 25% total boost.

This boost is applied multiplicatively on top of your base mining rate. If your base mining rate is 1.0 ZYM per hour, a full Security Circle brings that to 1.25 ZYM per hour. Over the course of a month, that difference amounts to 180 ZYM simply from having a full circle versus no circle. Over six months, that is over 1,000 additional ZYM — and this is before accounting for any other boosts like referral teams or streak bonuses.

The boost is described as "permanent" because it does not expire on a schedule — but it is contingent on your circle members remaining active. An inactive member's contribution may be reduced or paused, which is why you should actively monitor and maintain your circle. A full, active circle consistently delivering the full 25% is worth significantly more than a stale circle where some members have stopped mining.

What Happens When a Circle Member Is Inactive?

If one of your Security Circle members stops opening the app and ceases mining activity, their contribution to your boost is affected. The system is designed to reward active participation, not just historical connections. An account that has been dormant for an extended period is treated differently from an actively mining one.

In practical terms, if a circle member goes inactive, you will notice their slot may appear as "reduced" or "inactive" in your circle view. Your effective mining boost from that member drops accordingly. This is the system working as intended — it prevents users from building a circle of inactive friends and receiving a permanent boost without any of them actually participating.

The solution is simple: maintain awareness of your circle members' activity. If someone has stopped mining, reach out to them — a friendly message reminding them about the app is often enough to re-engage a lapsed miner. If they are truly gone for good, remove them from your circle and replace them with someone more active. You can remove and re-invite circle members at any time without penalty.

The Difference Between Security Circle and Referrals

This is one of the most commonly asked questions about Zyntex, and the confusion is understandable because both features involve inviting other users. Here is the key distinction:

Your Referral Team is a one-directional relationship — you invite someone using your referral code, they sign up, and you receive a mining rate boost for each active referral. There is no limit to how large your referral team can grow. You can refer one person or one thousand people, and each active referral contributes to your bonus. Referrals do not need to do anything with their own circle to benefit you.

Your Security Circle is a bidirectional trust relationship limited to exactly 5 people. Both parties must agree to the connection — you invite, they accept. The circle is smaller by design because it represents your inner ring of trusted contacts, not your broader network. The +5% per member boost from the circle is actually a higher per-person contribution than individual referral bonuses, reflecting the deeper trust relationship the circle represents.

Critically, the two systems are completely separate and stack on top of each other. A miner with a full Security Circle AND a large active referral team receives the boosts from both simultaneously. This is why experienced Zyntex miners prioritize filling their Security Circle first — it is the highest per-slot mining boost available — and then build their referral team on top of that foundation.

Best Practices for Choosing Circle Members

Prioritize Activity Over Relationship Closeness

Your best friend who downloaded Zyntex once and never opened it again is a worse Security Circle member than an acquaintance who mines every single day. When choosing your five circle members, prioritize users who you know are genuinely active on the platform. Check if they have maintained their login streak, if they discuss ZYM earnings with you, or if they regularly complete daily tasks. Activeness matters more than the closeness of your personal relationship.

Look for Users Who Have Referral Teams of Their Own

A circle member who also has a large active referral team is someone deeply invested in the Zyntex ecosystem. They have skin in the game and are unlikely to abandon the platform. These users make excellent circle members because their own financial stake in the network motivates them to stay engaged long-term.

Build a Communication Channel With Your Circle

The best Security Circles are ones where members actively communicate. Create a small group chat on WhatsApp or Telegram with your five circle members. Share mining tips, celebrate streak milestones, and encourage each other on days when engagement might lag. A connected circle stays active. A circle of strangers you never speak to will eventually go quiet.

Do Not Fill Empty Slots in a Rush

It is better to have three excellent active circle members than five mediocre ones. If you have filled all five slots with semi-active users, you may actually be better off removing the least active two and waiting for better candidates. Quality of circle members significantly impacts the real-world value of your 25% boost, because an inactive member delivers zero boost.

Frequently Asked Questions About the Security Circle

Can I be in someone's Security Circle without them being in mine?

Yes. The Security Circle is not required to be mutual. You can accept an invite from someone and join their circle without them joining yours. Each circle is independent — yours has 5 slots for people you trust, and each of your members has their own 5-slot circle which may or may not include you. This is a feature, not a limitation, because it allows the trust graph to form organically.

Does the boost apply 24/7 or only when I am actively mining?

The Security Circle boost applies to your mining rate at all times while your mining session is active, including overnight mining. Since Zyntex mines continuously during active sessions, the boost compounds around the clock — which is exactly why filling your circle as early as possible is so valuable.

What if I cannot find 5 people to add?

If you are new to Zyntex and your social circle has not yet joined, start by referring friends and family using your referral code. Once they are signed up and have created their accounts, you can invite them to your Security Circle. This is the natural flow for most users — build your referral team first, then convert your most active referrals into Security Circle members.

Can I be penalized for having circle members who turn out to be fake accounts?

The platform is designed to detect fraudulent accounts over time. If a circle member is found to be a bot or multiple-account violator, they may be removed from the network, which would automatically remove them from your circle. Your account itself would not be penalized for unknowingly adding a bad actor, as long as your own account is legitimate. This is another reason to only add people you personally know.
